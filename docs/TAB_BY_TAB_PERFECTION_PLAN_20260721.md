# CalledOut tab-by-tab perfection plan

## Product sentence

CalledOut turns a workout promise into a visible consequence: commit, prove it, or appear on The Wall.

Every primary screen must reinforce one part of that loop. Features that do not strengthen the loop should be removed, hidden, or moved deeper.

## The four-tab mental model

1. **Today — What do I owe today?**
   - The proof window, countdown, proof action, and redemption action live here.
   - The user should understand their next required action within two seconds.

2. **The Wall — What happens when someone misses?**
   - Misses are permanent records; redemption is a response, not an eraser.
   - Ranking must feel competitive without inviting harassment.

3. **Circles — Who will notice?**
   - Circles define the audience, rules, and people holding the user accountable.
   - Joining or creating a circle should clearly increase social consequence.

4. **Profile — What is my record?**
   - The profile is the durable scorecard: completion, misses, streaks, redemptions, history, and plan.
   - Subscription controls are secondary to the accountability record.

## Review order

1. Today
2. Commitment creation and proof capture
3. The Wall and member detail
4. Circles and circle detail
5. Profile, history, insights, and settings
6. Onboarding and paywall
7. Empty, loading, offline, error, and accessibility states

## Acceptance standard for every tab

- The purpose is clear without onboarding text.
- One primary action is visually dominant.
- No duplicate destinations or redundant metrics.
- Status copy says what happened and what happens next.
- Empty states teach the product rather than merely reporting no data.
- Free and Pro limits are visible only where they affect a decision.
- Every database mutation invalidates or refreshes the screens it changes.
- The interface uses native system typography and restrained motion.
